Passion Project

Project Description: I envision a platform for local artists of all types and skills (old, young, professional, amateur, painter, photograper, baker, knitter, etc.) to display their art work online to reach their community. It will be limited to the local area (i.e. 100 mile radius?) to encourage supporting the local art scene. It will provide a way for local artists to make money and share their creations/skills. It will be free for the artists to display their work and the site will generate revenue via ads that artists can opt into. It will differ from Etsy and other art sites by the local emphasis and free hosting and have a look similar to Pintrist.

Tourists and other visitors will be able to gain exposure to new art communities during their travels. I'm doing this partnership with Meow Wolf and CNM and coding it with Javascript.     Week 1 Summary

    Weeky 1 was an exciting and challenging first step into my coding career. We began the week familiarizing with the proper lexicon and toolchain that will be used. We proceeded to discuss and practice HTML, Javascript, CSS, and MySQL databases. The Snap Challenges tested our knowledge and I found myself overcomplicating the first exercise. We also began our Personal Web Project and Data Design Project. I am feeling pretty confident at this stage, but there have definitely been challenges. I enjoyed the monthly tech update at Fatpipe and will continue to attend in the future. It provides a great way to be exposed to local companies and network with other folks in the industry. I also met with my career buddy Adrian and we discussed our goals and ideas for after we graduate.

    Week 2 Summary

    Week 2 has been a challenging, but rewarding experience. The Snap Challenges forced me to dive deepr into Javascript, but I am feeling more confident each time. The Object Oriented Programming project has been incredibly difficult and took quite a bit of time to sort out the exceptions, but I successfully completed the first phase. The app challenge was really interesting to see and gave some insight on the quality of programs that can be created via the bootcamp as well as how to present your creations. I was impressed by the charity app and the foodtruck app in particular. The ad platform from Sandia was impressive, but it would be easy for Facebook and Google to offer the same services down the road.

    Week 3 Summary

    Week 3 has been a very successful and stimulating period. I really enjoy CSS and look forward to learning as much as I can about it. We voted on our capstone projects and I am excited to be given the opportunity to work on my idea for a local art platform. My teammates Will and Brandon are very knowledgeable and I am excited to work with them and learn from them! Our project is going to turn out awesome and I can't wait to showcase it to the class and industry peers. There is huge potential to continue the project after the class ends as a non-profit entity.

    Week 4 Summary

    It is hard to believe we are 40% done with the bootcamp already, but the list of new skills I have developed surprises me. I enjoyed learning Bootstrap this week and it feels like a game-changer for styling a site. It was humbling to see George's solutions for the CSS activity because he accomplished the same result, if not better, than my solution in 1/5 the amount of lines of code. I had breakfast and lunch multiple times with my capstone team as well as going to my teammate Brandon's house to collaborate on our project. The database setup, particularly the unit testing, is a challenge, but it will set the right foundation for our project to be successful. I endorsed several classmates' skills on LinkedIn and continue to expand my network. I updated my resume based on Dan's feedback. The pro dev guest speakers on Friday were amazing, particularly the inspiring Chales Ashley who I intend to network extensively with. It has been a good week and I look forward to continuing the capstone project.

    The Forbidden Island board game was a great team activity! Brandon, Will, and I managed to win the game as the water levels got perilously high and the land mass was tiny. We worked very well together and communicated our thoughts effectively throughout the game. It reinforced my awareness of my questionable verbal communication skills as I struggled to express my thoughts, but my team was patient with me and it worked out well. I realize I need to practice my team communication to ensure optimal cooperation. I am a much better written communicator than verbal and will voice my ideas via Slack if all else fails.

    Week 5 Summary

    It is amazing to consider we are halfway done with the bootcamp already. Most of the week was focused on the capstone project, specifically unit testing. I am enjoying MySQL and PHP and I'm gaining a better understanding of these tools. I had some challenges getting the unit testing working properly, but I was able to code the tests for our Profile class and get everything working. I spent a late night Friday getting another two classes to pass their tests cleanly. We need to add some additional tests to those classes and get the final class cleaned up and working correctly. My team and I are meeting Sunday to continue working on our project. We are very excited for the potential for this project and we work great together. I received feedback on my wireframes for the PWP and everything is looking good so far. I will be working hard on that project this weekend as well. It was a good week and I am proud of what we have accomplished.
